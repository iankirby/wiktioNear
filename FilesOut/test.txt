<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Usage_notes">Usage notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=also&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Usage notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The adverb <b>also</b> can go in various places within a sentence, with minimal change of meaning.
</p>
<ul><li><div class="h-usage-example"><i class="Latn mention e-example" lang="en">The golf courses are excellent. <b>Also</b>, the tennis courts are fantastic.</i></div></li>
<li><div class="h-usage-example"><i class="Latn mention e-example" lang="en">The golf courses are excellent and the tennis courts are fantastic <b>also</b>.</i></div></li>
<li><div class="h-usage-example"><i class="Latn mention e-example" lang="en">Not only are the golf courses excellent but <b>also</b> the tennis courts are fantastic.</i></div></li>
<li><div class="h-usage-example"><i class="Latn mention e-example" lang="en">There are not just excellent golf courses here. There are <b>also</b> fantastic tennis courts.</i></div></li></ul>
